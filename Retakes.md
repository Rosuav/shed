Zombie Retakes
==============

Backstory
---------

It's the zombie apocalypse. You hear of hordes of people being zombified where
they live, work, play, always by contact and often quite swiftly. People try to
embrace their loved ones, and an entire family turns in mere minutes. Brainless
office workers become completely brainless, spreading the brainlessness through
the entire office in less than an hour.

And they're aggressive. They don't merely wander along; they actively seek out
living people and mob them. They're uncoordinated but terrifying.

You are isolated, giving you a strange form of protection for a while. But soon
your supplies run low, and it's necessary to risk going out into the city for
more. Nipping into abandoned shops, looting the homes of the undead, you keep
yourself going for just one more day at a time. Until finally it happens: a
zombie catches you by surprise, and grabs you to bite down hard...

... only, it doesn't end that way. In that crisis of your life, you discover an
amazing ability you never thought you had. (Yeah I know, never heard THAT one
before.) A short-range teleportation ability! You vanish from under the jaws of
the zombie, and looking around, find that you're now a few meters away from a
zombie who has now completely lost track of you.

You try out your new ability a few more times, quickly finding its limitations;
it moves you only a short distance, and it's impossible to choose the precise
direction, but you can at least teleport either "forwards" or "backwards". But
you'll take whatever advantage you can. After all, most zombie horror survivors
get to team up with three other people (why no more than that? Nobody knows),
and you're here alone. But what powers your peculiar ability? You have no idea
how you figured this out, but - zombie brains. Yep. You have to keep on killing
zombies to power it up. Cool. Let's do this.

Gameplay
--------

Conquest levels move the plot forward. There might be other types of level but
these are the most important. In them, you enter a location inhabited by a few
enemies. Early levels have you going into houses, then boarded-up buildings,
and by the mid game, you're assaulting what was clearly intended to be a fort
where survivors hang out. At the end of each conquest mission, the building is
described as having been retaken.

You have only a melee attack. Fortunately, so do your enemies. You need to be
swift, aggressive, and agile to defeat them; or you can move around them. To
retake the location, you must reach certain key points within it, and spend
time interacting with them; your enemies can do the same to undo your progress,
but it takes them three times as long as it takes you. You can also win by
eliminating the enemies in the area, as they generally do not respawn in the
time frame of a mission; however this is a very risky strategy.

As can be expected, your enemies are quite dumb, often attacking next to you
rather than at you. But if they do attack you, well, you don't have hitpoints,
so you emergency teleport - assuming you have enough charge to do so. If you
don't, game over, reload from the start of the mission. After each level, you
get statistics on the number of enemies defeated, the number of hops performed,
and the number of emergency hops taken (which drastically lower your score).

Lore
----

Each time you defeat a conquest level, you get to find out what happened at
that location. It was a small group of survivors (later on, a fort) that lasted
through a number of zombie attacks. Some of the attacks managed to get inside.
But alas, in the final attack, the zombies managed to break down several key
defensive locations (the exact number that you had to deal with in order to
retake it), and they abandoned it, leaving behind the slain. You feel good for
reclaiming fortresses from the zombies, and begin to hope that you could maybe
conquer the entire city!

This lore dump is delivered unexplainedly, since there's no way you'll find a
note left behind saying why they left. It's just the post-level win screen.

Nobody has any guns here. There might be some lore/flavour notes about gun
control laws and their impact on zombie apocalypses, but you never find any
sort of firearm or even a crossbow. It's all melee.

The Reveal
----------

You're a zombie. You're not retaking fortresses that the zombies took... you're
conquering fortresses still held by living people. Those reports of the fort
being abandoned are what just happened right then as you were there.

Your teleportation ability is actually just a focus shift to another zombie. An
emergency teleportation happens whenever a human kills the zombie we had focus
on, and a manual teleportation is a simple focus switch.

The dumbness of your enemies is because, even though you aren't aware of them,
there are myriad other zombies all around you, and they're dealing with them as
well as you. You are, after all, no different from any other.

If it's possible, it would be great to subtly allude to this, by having the
early levels involve slower but more precise enemies (seldom attacking where
you're not, but not as quick to move or attack, making them easier to bite),
but in the later ones, where you're fighting hardened defenders, they are fast
and "inaccurate" (since they're constantly bashing in heads, just not yours).

The Conclusion
--------------

After you retake the final fortress, you climb a gigantic lookout tower and
gaze over the city. It is clean, not a single enemy in sight. Magnificent. You
focus down into the city, and teleport there, zipping around through repeated
teleportations, revelling in your ability and your accomplishments...

from one zombie to another. The city has been destroyed. Nobody is left alive.
