Finding Saint Tordesillas

In 1494, the Treaty of Tordesillas was organized by the Pope to draw a separation between Castilian (Spanish) and Portuguese claims in the New World. Evidence of this can still be seen, as a large number of cities and towns have been named in honour of various saints - but these names reflect the languages predominant in these areas. For example, "San Antonio" (Saint Anthony) is Spanish, but "São José" (Saint Joseph) is Portuguese. We shall attempt to illustrate this distinction by placing map markers for every known city with a saint name.
